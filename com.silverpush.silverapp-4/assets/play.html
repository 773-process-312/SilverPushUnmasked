Playcez is the next generation of local discovery. Built on proprietary, soon-to-be-patented technology, Playcez mines social media data in real-time to provide location-specific suggestions based on who is saying what, where. Whether they are looking to eat, play or shop, Playcez is set to become the tool of choice for India is mobile-savvy youth.

Abhishekheads technology and user experience at PlayCez. He loves to combine research with the latest gadgets, and has worked on a lot of cool tech from hacking Kinect to insert a presenter persona into a presentation, to building a real-time augmented reality word translator app, and using cameras to make paper replicas produce sound just like the real thing. Before PlayCez he co-founded an iPhone app consulting firm that successfully built apps for 3rd party clients, including an iPad trading plaftorm, having had his appetite for start-ups whetted when he incubated a company, Innosium, while at IIT Kanpur. With stints in between helping to build an information system for Goldman Sach internal traders and as a consultant at TCS Innovation Labs, where he researched 3D data and image processing, Abhishek is passionate about building the best experience for Playcez users.


Ashwani is a hustler and the CEO of PlayCez. Driven by pure passion, he can be found working on everything from business development to product development to sales, to keep things moving upwards. While a student at IIT Kanpur, he dabbled in music, electronics and even taekwondo, before being introduced to the startup world by two seniors who came back from the US to start a company at the IITK incubation center. Ashwani was the first hire at the company, which is now the largest aftermarket software provider for Blackberry Devices. He later founded his first startup in their office, a content aggregation platform, scaling it up to 5,000 users. Subsequently, he joined Deloitte as a consultant in Hyderabad for 2.5 years to get experience in business strategy, and then left to work on a product called Qontext, which was later acquired by Autodesk. In 2010, he was ready to go it alone again, and founded PlayCez. He has bootstrapped the startup since and continues with the conviction and motivation to disrupt the way people discover local places.
